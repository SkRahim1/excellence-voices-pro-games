# Master Curriculum Specification: Excellence Voices Pro

This document defines the complete year-long (36-Week) progressive syllabus and the separate 12-Week Master Grammar Track. It is structured to take a student from absolute zero (non-speaker) to a professional English communicator.

---

## Part 1: Immersive Learning Game Specs

### Game 1: "Adventure Path" (Choice-Based Story Game)
* **Mechanic:** Animated, audio-narrated stories pause at critical plot branches. The student must read and speak their chosen path aloud (e.g., *"I will search the left room"*). The speech engine registers pronunciation and branches the story.
* **Syllabus Integration:** Story content mirrors the weekly vocabulary and idioms.

### Game 2: "Roleplay Arena" (Avatar Conversations)
* **Mechanic:** Simulated real-world environments where students dialogue with AI characters (e.g., Doctor, Principal, Shopkeeper, Interviewer). Evaluates responsiveness, grammatical structure, and pacing.

### Game 3: "Story Builder" (Creative Speech Generation)
* **Mechanic:** The AI gives the student a story-starter and 3 key terms (including target phrasal verbs or idioms). The student has 30 seconds to speak the next sentence. The engine transcribes it and appends it to the story.

## Part 2: The Master 36-Week Curriculum (Zero to Pro)

### Quarter 1: Receptive & Foundation Stage (Weeks 1 to 9)
* **Objective:** Building phonetic recognition, elementary commands, expressing physical needs, and simple 3-4 word sentences.

* **Week 1: Alphabet Sounds & Phonics Foundations**
  * *Focus:* Native letter pronunciation, vowel vs. consonant vocal placement, and breathing control.
  * *Activity:* Sound imitation drills matching letters to visual animations.
* **Week 2: Short and Long Vowels**
  * *Focus:* Vowel sounds (e.g., cap/cape, pin/pine, hop/hope) and silent letters (write, know, listen).
  * *Activity:* Sound Matcher game checking vocal clarity.
* **Week 3: Classroom Vocabulary & Commands**
  * *Focus:* Action verbs (sit, stand, write, read, open, close). Polite words (please, sorry, thank you).
  * *Activity:* "Simon Says" verbal action game.
* **Week 4: Survival English & Simple Needs**
  * *Focus:* Request phrases: *"May I go to..."*, *"Can you give me..."*, *"I need help."*
  * *Activity:* Roleplay Arena: Asking the teacher for a pencil or permission to drink water.
* **Week 5: Core Nouns & Objects (School & Home)**
  * *Focus:* Board, desk, window, chair, clock, plate, glass, bed, door.
  * *Activity:* Naming items in a virtual bedroom.
* **Week 6: Colors, Sizes, & Shapes (Adjectives)**
  * *Focus:* Big, tiny, long, short, round, square, red, yellow, green.
  * *Activity:* Simple descriptions: *"I see a big blue balloon."*
* **Week 7: Self & Family Introductions**
  * *Focus:* Family relationships (father, mother, brother, sister, cousin). Introducing age and name.
  * *Activity:* Speaking intro: *"Hello, I am Rahul. This is my mother."*
* **Week 8: Daily Routines & Time-of-Day Nouns**
  * *Focus:* Morning, afternoon, night, brush, wash, play, sleep, eat.
  * *Activity:* Sequencing routines: *"In the morning, I eat breakfast."*
* **Week 9: Quarter 1 Capstone Assessment**
  * *Focus:* Verification of basic pronunciation, phonetic accuracy, and survival requests. Unlocks Level 1 Certificate.

---

### Quarter 2: Expressive & Sentence-Building Stage (Weeks 10 to 18)
* **Objective:** Expanding vocabulary, using prepositions, forming questions, and expressing basic thoughts.

* **Week 10: Likes, Dislikes & Hobbies**
  * *Focus:* *"I enjoy..."*, *"I am interested in..."*, *"I dislike..."*. Verbs in gerund form (reading, swimming).
  * *Activity:* Describing free-time activities to the AI tutor.
* **Week 11: Prepositions of Place & Navigation**
  * *Focus:* In, on, under, between, next to, behind, in front of.
  * *Activity:* "Where is the key?" object description drill.
* **Week 12: Calendar, Weather, & Seasons**
  * *Focus:* Days, months, seasons, hot, windy, foggy, raining, snowy.
  * *Activity:* Daily weather reporter roleplay.
* **Week 13: Animal Kingdoms & Action Words**
  * *Focus:* Wings, tail, paws, fly, jump, crawl, bark, roar.
  * *Activity:* Describing animal behaviors: *"A monkey climbs trees."*
* **Week 14: Core Phrasal Verbs (Daily Actions)**
  * *Focus:* Wake up, get up, put on, take off, turn on, turn off.
  * *Activity:* Roleplay: Talking through steps of getting ready for school.
* **Week 15: Forming Simple Questions (Wh- Questions)**
  * *Focus:* Asking: *"What is...", "Where is...", "Who is..."*
  * *Activity:* Mystery card game: Ask questions to identify a hidden object.
* **Week 16: Describing Continuous Actions**
  * *Focus:* Present continuous tense (*"is running"*, *"are playing"*).
  * *Activity:* Describing busy scenes: *"The children are playing football."*
* **Week 17: Basic Collocations & Word Pairs**
  * *Focus:* Make a mistake, take a photo, do homework, tell a story, have fun.
  * *Activity:* Sentence construction using correct verb-noun pairs.
* **Week 18: Quarter 2 Capstone Assessment**
  * *Focus:* Conversational matching, fluency assessment, and simple question formation.

---

### Quarter 3: Conversational Fluency & Social Stage (Weeks 19 to 27)
* **Objective:** Handling social situations, using idioms, phrasal verbs, and active listening expressions.

* **Week 19: Greeting Peers & Small Talk**
  * *Focus:* Greetings (*"How have you been?", "Nice to meet you"*). Response markers (*"Really?", "That's great!"*).
  * *Activity:* Simulated school-yard chat with a new classmate.
* **Week 20: Giving and Asking for Directions**
  * *Focus:* Turn left, go straight, cross the street, landmark descriptors, distance.
  * *Activity:* Roleplay Arena: Guiding a visitor to the principal's office.
* **Week 21: Conversational Idioms (Part 1)**
  * *Focus:* *Piece of cake, break a leg, call it a day, once in a blue moon, under the weather*.
  * *Activity:* Adventure Path: Using idioms correctly in dialogues.
* **Week 22: Phrasal Verbs (Social & School life)**
  * *Focus:* Look for, pick up, drop off, write down, hand in, look up.
  * *Activity:* Dialogues: Asking a teacher about handing in assignments.
* **Week 23: Telephone Etiquette & Messaging**
  * *Focus:* Taking messages, asking to speak to someone, polite endings.
  * *Activity:* Phone call simulation: Inviting a friend to a weekend game.
* **Week 24: Health, Symptoms & Absence Reasons**
  * *Focus:* Sore throat, cough, fever, stomach ache, explain absence (*"I was unable to attend due to..."*).
  * *Activity:* Simulating a call to the class teacher explaining an illness.
* **Week 25: Future Plans & Goals**
  * *Focus:* *"I plan to..."*, *"My goal is to..."*, *"In the long run..."*.
  * *Activity:* Speed presentation: Sharing your dream job.
* **Week 26: Making Comparisons (Adjectives & Qualifiers)**
  * *Focus:* Comparatives/superlatives. Modifying words: *much better, slightly bigger, by far the best*.
  * *Activity:* Product review speaking task: Comparing two school bags or toys.
* **Week 27: Quarter 3 Capstone Assessment**
  * *Focus:* Multi-turn conversation logic, correct usage of idioms and phrasal verbs. Unlocks Level 2 Certificate.

---

### Quarter 4: Presentation, Rhetoric, & Pro Stage (Weeks 28 to 36)
* **Objective:** Public speaking, presentation structures, argument formation, and advanced idioms.

* **Week 28: Storytelling & Narrative Hooking**
  * *Focus:* Chronological transitions (*"all of a sudden," "in the end"*), suspense building, vocal range.
  * *Activity:* Story Builder: Relaying a personal adventure story.
* **Week 29: Structure of a Presentation (Hook, Body, Conclusion)**
  * *Focus:* Intro transitions (*"Today I will speak about..."*), body linking, and calls to action.
  * *Activity:* Creating a 1.5-minute speech on *"Why Books are Better than Screens."*
* **Week 30: Voice Modulation & Speed Control**
  * *Focus:* Stressing key nouns/verbs, pausing for suspense, volume control, speaking at 110-130 WPM.
  * *Activity:* Teleprompter reading test with live pacing feedback.
* **Week 31: Advanced Idioms & Metaphors (Professional English)**
  * *Focus:* *Hit the nail on the head, on the same page, burn the midnight oil, blessing in disguise*.
  * *Activity:* Describing academic achievements using idioms.
* **Week 32: Body Language & Stage Presence**
  * *Focus:* Eye contact prompts, hand gestures, upright stance.
  * *Activity:* Practice speech recording with posture targets.
* **Week 33: Debate Structure & Argumentation**
  * *Focus:* Point-Explanation-Evidence (P-E-E). Rebuttal phrases: *"While I understand that point, I disagree because..."*
  * *Activity:* Debate the AI: *"Should exams be banned?"*
* **Week 34: Explaining Complex Issues (Problem-Solution Framework)**
  * *Focus:* Defining a problem, explaining causes, and presenting 3 logical solutions.
  * *Activity:* Group leader presentation simulation.
* **Week 35: Impromptu Speaking (Extempore mastery)**
  * *Focus:* Structuring thoughts under 15 seconds, using filler transitions gracefully (*"That is an interesting topic, let me start by..."*).
  * *Activity:* Sudden-death topic challenge.
* **Week 36: Final Capstone Graduation Assessment**
  * *Focus:* 3-minute formal speech evaluated on Pronunciation, Vocabulary, Grammar, Pacing, and Confidence. Unlocks Excellence Voices Pro Master Communicator Certificate.

## Part 3: 12-Week (3-Month) Master Grammar Module

This intensive track focuses on eliminating errors in speech. It emphasizes **sentence formation, question structure, modals, and word order**.

### Week 1: Subject-Verb Agreement (The Core Rule)
* **Objective:** Consistent singular/plural matching. Eliminating *"He play"* or *"They writes"*.
* **Drills:** Speaking exercises replacing singular nouns with pronouns and matching verbs in real-time.

### Week 2: Articles & Noun Plurality
* **Objective:** Mastery of *"a, an, the"* and singular/plural nouns.
* **Drills:** Identifying countable vs. uncountable items and speaking correct qualifiers (*"a box of," "many books"*).

### Week 3: Pronouns & Possessive Forms
* **Objective:** Eliminating pronoun errors (*"him is happy"*, *"me friend told me"*).
* **Drills:** Reading scripts replacing nouns with correct pronouns (subjective, objective, and possessive).

### Week 4: Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) Word Order
* **Objective:** Correct syntax in basic, compound, and complex sentences.
* **Drills:** Sentence unscrambling and speaking the correct word order.

### Week 5: Present Tenses (Simple vs. Continuous)
* **Objective:** Using simple present for habits (*"I study"*) and present continuous for current actions (*"I am studying"*).
* **Drills:** Prompt matching: *"What do you do?"* vs. *"What are you doing?"*

### Week 6: Past Tense Conjugations (Regular & Irregular)
* **Objective:** Correct past forms. Eliminating mistakes like *"I goed"* or *"I see him yesterday"*.
* **Drills:** "Yesterday Game": Describing a past day using 10 target irregular verbs.

### Week 7: Future Tense Formations (Will vs. Going to)
* **Objective:** Formulating future thoughts accurately based on intention vs. instant decision.
* **Drills:** Expressing weather predictions and travel schedules.

### Week 8: Question Structure (Yes/No Questions)
* **Objective:** Word inversion in closed questions: **Auxiliary Verb + Subject + Main Verb** (*"Do you walk?"*, *"Is she eating?"*).
* **Drills:** Interactive Q&A asking the AI Tutor questions about their day.

### Week 9: Question Structure (Wh- Questions)
* **Objective:** Word order in open questions: **Wh- word + Auxiliary + Subject + Verb** (eliminating *"Where you are?"* in favor of *"Where are you?"*).
* **Drills:** Interviewing NPCs by generating structured Wh- questions.

### Week 10: Negation Rules
* **Objective:** Using *"don't, doesn't, didn't"* correctly and avoiding double negatives.
* **Drills:** Correcting incorrect negative statements spoken by the AI.

### Week 11: Prepositions of Place and Time
* **Objective:** Correct use of *"in, on, at, to, for"*.
* **Drills:** Describing dates, times, and maps.

### Week 12: Comprehensive Modals of Speaking
* **Objective:** Mastering modals for permissions (*can, may*), requests (*could, would*), recommendations (*should, ought to*), and requirements (*must, have to*).
* **Drills:** Roleplay: Negotiating politely with a teacher using modals (e.g., *"Would it be possible to submit tomorrow?"*).
